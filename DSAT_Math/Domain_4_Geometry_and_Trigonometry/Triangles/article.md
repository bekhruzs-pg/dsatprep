# Triangles

## 1. Overview
Triangles are foundational to geometry. The DSAT tests your understanding of triangle properties, special right triangles (30-60-90, 45-45-90), and the Pythagorean theorem. These questions often involve finding missing sides or angles.

## 2. Key Concepts & Formulas
*   **Angle Sum**: The angles in a triangle always add to $180°$.
*   **Pythagorean Theorem**: For a **right triangle**: $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$ (where $c$ is the hypotenuse).
*   **Special Right Triangles**:
    *   **45-45-90**: Sides are in the ratio $1 : 1 : \sqrt{2}$.
    *   **30-60-90**: Sides are in the ratio $1 : \sqrt{3} : 2$ (opposite the 30°, 60°, and 90° angles respectively).
*   **Similar Triangles**: Same shape, different size. Corresponding sides are proportional.
*   **Congruent Triangles**: Same shape, same size. Corresponding sides and angles are equal.

## 3. Step-by-Step Strategy
1.  **Identify the Type**: Is it a right triangle? Isosceles? Equilateral?
2.  **Label What You Know**: Mark given sides and angles.
3.  **Apply the Right Formula**:
    *   Right triangle → Pythagorean theorem or special right triangle ratios.
    *   Angles → Use the $180°$ rule.
4.  **Similar Triangles**: Set up proportions. $\frac{a_1}{a_2} = \frac{b_1}{b_2}$.

## 4. Common Pitfalls
*   **Hypotenuse Confusion**: The hypotenuse is **always** the longest side and is **opposite** the right angle.
*   **Special Triangle Order**: In 30-60-90, the side opposite 30° is the shortest, not the side opposite 60°.
*   **Units**: Make sure all sides are in the same units before using the Pythagorean theorem.

## 5. Example Walkthrough
**Problem**: A right triangle has legs of length 6 and 8. What is the length of the hypotenuse?

**Solution**:
1.  **Identify**: Right triangle. Pythagorean theorem.
2.  **Formula**: $a^2 + b^2 = c^2$
3.  **Plug in**: $6^2 + 8^2 = c^2$
4.  **Calculate**: $36 + 64 = c^2 \rightarrow 100 = c^2$
5.  **Solve**: $c = 10$
6.  **Answer**: The hypotenuse is 10.
