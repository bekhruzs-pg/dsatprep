# Circles

## 1. Overview
Circle questions test your knowledge of the circle equation, arc length, sector area, and the relationship between radius, diameter, and circumference. Desmos is especially useful for graphing circle equations to find the center and radius visually.

## 2. Key Concepts & Formulas
*   **Equation of a Circle**: $(x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2$
    *   Center: $(h, k)$
    *   Radius: $r$
*   **Circumference**: $C = 2\pi r$ or $C = \pi d$
*   **Area**: $A = \pi r^2$
*   **Arc Length**: $\text{Arc} = \frac{\theta}{360°} \times 2\pi r$ (for degrees) or $\text{Arc} = \theta \times r$ (for radians)
*   **Sector Area**: $\text{Sector} = \frac{\theta}{360°} \times \pi r^2$ (for degrees)

## 3. Step-by-Step Strategy
1.  **Identify What's Given**: Radius? Diameter? Center coordinates?
2.  **Circle Equation**: If given $(x-3)^2 + (y+2)^2 = 16$, the center is $(3, -2)$ and $r^2 = 16$, so $r = 4$.
3.  **Arc/Sector**: First find the fraction of the circle ($\theta/360°$), then multiply by the full circumference or area.
4.  **Desmos**: Graph the circle equation to visually confirm the center and radius.

## 4. Common Pitfalls
*   **Sign Errors in Center**: $(x - 3)^2$ means the center x-coordinate is $+3$, NOT $-3$.
*   **Radius vs. Diameter**: Mixing up $r$ and $d$ in formulas. Remember $d = 2r$.
*   **Degrees vs. Radians**: Arc length formula changes depending on the unit of the angle.

## 5. Example Walkthrough
**Problem**: A circle has the equation $(x + 1)^2 + (y - 4)^2 = 9$. What is the radius?

**Solution**:
1.  **Standard Form**: $(x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2$
2.  **Rewrite**: $(x - (-1))^2 + (y - 4)^2 = 9$
3.  **Identify**: $r^2 = 9$
4.  **Solve**: $r = 3$
5.  **Answer**: The radius is 3.
