# Ratios, Rates, and Proportions

## 1. Overview
This topic is all about relationships between quantities. Whether it's converting currency, scaling a recipe, or calculating speed, the core skill is maintaining the correct ratio. The DSAT often embeds these in wordy real-world scenarios.

## 2. Key Concepts & Formulas
*   **Ratio**: A comparison of two quantities (e.g., $a:b$ or $a/b$).
*   **Proportion**: An equation stating that two ratios are equal ($\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$).
*   **Rate**: A ratio of two different units (e.g., miles per hour).
*   **Unit Conversion**: Multiplying by a conversion factor (e.g., $\frac{60 \text{ min}}{1 \text{ hr}}$) to change units without changing value.

## 3. Step-by-Step Strategy
1.  **Identify Units**: Underline the units given and the units asked for.
2.  **Set up Proportion**: Match units on both sides.
    *   $\frac{\text{miles}}{\text{hour}} = \frac{\text{miles}}{\text{hour}}$
3.  **Dimensional Analysis**: For multi-step conversions, chain them together so units cancel out.
    *   $5 \text{ km} \times \frac{1000 \text{ m}}{1 \text{ km}} \times \frac{100 \text{ cm}}{1 \text{ m}} = ...$
4.  **Cross-Multiply**: If $\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}$, then $ad = bc$.

## 4. Common Pitfalls
*   **Mismatched Units**: Setting up $\frac{\text{miles}}{\text{hour}} = \frac{\text{hour}}{\text{miles}}$. Keep numerators and denominators consistent.
*   **Squared/Cubed Units**: When converting area or volume, remember to square/cube the conversion factor.
    *   $1 \text{ ft} = 12 \text{ in}$, but $1 \text{ ft}^2 = 144 \text{ in}^2$.

## 5. Example Walkthrough
**Problem**: A map has a scale of 1 inch = 50 miles. If two cities are 3.5 inches apart on the map, how far apart are they in reality?

**Solution**:
1.  **Set up Proportion**: $\frac{1 \text{ inch}}{50 \text{ miles}} = \frac{3.5 \text{ inches}}{x \text{ miles}}$
2.  **Cross-Multiply**: $1 \cdot x = 50 \cdot 3.5$
3.  **Solve**: $x = 175$
4.  **Answer**: 175 miles.
